Synopsis

African Heads of State launched the Africa Adaptation Initiative (AAI) to ensure the Continent urgently adapts to the adverse effects of climate change in the immediate, short, medium and longer terms. The AAI launch event took place in Paris during the 21st Conference of the Parties (COP) in December 2015. AAI will be implemented in 3 phases

Goals

The AAI aims to enhance action on adaptation, with the aim of addressing the adaptation financing gap, and implementing measures to address disaster risk reduction and resilience needs in Africa.

Objectives

a) Raise awareness of climate adaptation; b) Facilitate knowledge management, capacity building and capacity strengthening; c) Support and facilitate resource mobilization for implementation; d) Promote co-operation and partnerships (at sub-regional and regional levels) for synergy, scale and maximize shared benefits; e) Track progress through monitoring and evaluation of action.

CSA Activities and interventions

Climate Smart Crops/Promotion of drought tolerant crops; Climate smart livestock practices/Livestock feed management/Improved breeds; Crop Disease and pest management Livestock disease and pest management; Markets, credit/finance and value chains for CSA; Policy and Advocacy; Postharvest management; Renewable energy for CSA; Smart water management; Soil management; Weather & Agroadvisory Services; Capacity Building; Tools and Methodologies; Insurance
