Synopsis

Over the last decade, IDRC has supported several capacity building efforts in the field of climate change in Africa. Despite the significant impacts these programs have had, the representation of women in these efforts has been small, especially women from Francophone countries. This is due to weak technical capacity to compete in open calls, lack of confidence to compete with men on an equal footing, and language barriers, among other factors.

Goals

This project is a fellowship program designed to respond to the need for strengthening women’s capacities in climate change research, innovation, and policy in Francophone West Africa.

Objectives

The overall objective is to develop the climate change leadership capacity of 18 early to mid-career women researchers, innovators, and policy advisors in Francophone West Africa to advance and apply knowledge in climate change adaptation and resilience.
