Synopsis

The Kenya Climate Smart Agriculture Strategy 2017-2026 (KCSAS) aims to build the resilience of agricultural systems, and minimize emissions for enhanced food and nutritional security and improved livelihoods.

Goals

To facilitate agriculture that sustainably increases productivity, enhances resilience and minimizes greenhouse gas emissions.

Objectives

a) enhance the adaptive capacity and resilience of farmers, pastoralists, and fisher-folk to the adverse impacts of climate change; b) develop mechanisms that minimize greenhouse gas emissions from agricultural production systems; c) create an enabling regulatory and institutional framework; d) address cross-cutting issues that adversely impact climate smart agriculture.

CSA Activities and interventions

All CSA interventions: Agroforestry; Climate Smart Crops; Climate smart livestock practices; Livestock feed management; Crop Disease and pest management; Livestock disease and pest management; Markets, credit/finance and value chains for CSA; Policy and Advocacy; Postharvest management; Renewable energy for CSA; Smart water management; Soil management; Weather & Agroadvisory Services; Capacity Building; Tools and Methodologies; Insurance
