Climate-Smart Brachiaria Grasses for Improving Livestock Production in East Africa

Synopsis

The program is contributing to food and feed security and poverty reduction in East Africa through climate-smart Brachiaria grasses and capacity building in improved tropical forage technology, natural resource management, and adaptation and mitigation of climate change

Goals

To be updated

Objectives

a) Determine the role of endophytes (symbiotic microorganisms within the plants) in improving adaptation of Brachiaria grasses to climate change (drought) and develop novel methods to detect endophytes; b) Quantify the contribution of drought and low soil fertility in adapted Brachiaria grasses to the mitigation of climate change; c) Integrate improved Brachiara grasses in mixed smallholder crop-livestock systems and determine their role in improving milk and meat production in grazing and cut-and-carry forage systems while reducing land degradation; d) Facilitate the creation of forage seed production enterprises with guaranteed markets among poor farmers, mainly female farmers
