Synopsis

FICCF is promoting the transition to CSA through support to microfinance institutions (MFIs) which are working in partnership with agribusiness aggregators and smallholder farmers to adopt a range of tools, technologies and practices to facilitate adaptation and increase resilience to an increasingly variable climate.

Goals

The goal is to support a private sector response to CSA investments for adaptation / resilience with low carbon benefits.

Objectives

To support the scaling up of innovation and investment in adaptation/ resilience and low carbon products, services and assets in the agricultural sector by providing repayable grants to micro-finance institutions (MFIs) and their agribusiness partners for on-lending to farmers and agribusiness actors engaged in the production of staple commodities in Kenya.

Comments

The Finance Innovation for Climate Change Fund (FICCF) is a component of the DFID Kenya program Strengthening Adaptation and Resilience to Climate Change in Kenya (StARCK+) which aims to focus its resources in (a) catalyzing private sector innovation and investment, (b) climate change governance, focusing on stronger policy, institutional and regulatory framework, and (c) enhancing capacity of civil society.
