Synopsis

The National Strategy for Growth and Reduction of Poverty (NSGRP II or MKUKUTA II) is an organizing framework to rally national efforts in accelerating poverty-reducing growth. It is a second in series of results – based national strategy.

Goals

a)Pursuing sound macroeconomic management; b)Reducing income poverty through promoting inclusive, sustainable,and employment-enhancing growth;c)Ensuring creation of productive and decent employment, especially for women and youth;d)Ensuring food security and climate change adaptation and mitigation;e)Allocating and utilizing national resources equitably and efficiently for growth and poverty reduction, especially in rural areas 

Objectives

a)To ensure timely availability of reliable and adequate data for monitoring growth and poverty reduction; b)To enhance storage, retrieval, access, and use of data by a wide array of development stakeholders; c)To carry out detailed analysis of data on growth and poverty trends;d)To disseminate the findings of research and analysis of data to a wide array of stakeholders;e)To promote evidence –based planning, budgeting, and decision making at all levels of government;f)To promote evidence-based dialogue among development stakeholders;g)To ensure that regional and international targets ratified by Tanzania are integrated into national development targets and monitored as part of MKUKUTA II monitoring 

CSA Activities and interventions

Climate Smart Crops/Promotion of drought tolerant crops; Climate smart livestock practices/Livestock feed management/Improved breeds; Crop Disease and pest management; Livestock disease and pest management; Markets, credit/finance and value chains for CSA; Policy and Advocacy; Postharvest management; Renewable energy for CSA; Smart water management; Soil management; Weather & Agroadvisory Services; Capacity Building; Tools and Methodologies; Insurance
