Synopsis

On 13 November 2019, the the Ministry of Environment of Rwanda (MoE) received a grant to develop a scale-up proposal for the project titled: “Reducing Vulnerability to Climate Change in North West Rwanda through Community Based Adaptation”

Goals

enhance capacities of local communities to access climate finance to scale up climate change adaptation technologies that were tested during the Project “Reducing Vulnerability to Climate Change in North West Rwanda through Community Based Adaptation: RV3CBA”.

Objectives

a) Adaptation to climate change (rainfall intensity and duration) through integrated land and water management to support climate-resilient production and post-harvest systems, b) Support for the transition from exploitive farming practices to sustainable diversified livelihoods, c) Capacity building of local institutions to improve understanding of climate change impacts and scale up effective adaptation strategies at the local level.

CSA Activities and interventions

Climate Smart Crops/Promotion of drought tolerant crops; Climate smart livestock practices/Livestock feed management/Improved breeds; Crop Disease and pest management; Livestock disease and pest management; Markets, credit/finance and value chains for CSA; Policy and Advocacy; Postharvest management; Renewable energy for CSA; Smart water management; Soil management; Weather & Agroadvisory Services; Capacity Building; Tools and Methodologies; Insurance
